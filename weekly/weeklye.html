Recently, I discovered an interesting short story called 17776. This story is really something that motivates me to continue learning about how to use HTML and CSS to tell a tale.

The tl;dr is that humanity stopped dying in 2026 and since then society has basically remained the same.

Humans had developed the technology necessary to make sure nobody dies and solve every problem, but people didn't want to explore the stars. Why?


Well, as the story puts it, you would eventually run out of resources to explore, and destroy the Earth in the pursuit of raw materials.

And so now everyone lives in a stagnant world, by choice. The end state of humanity is one of play.

Oh yeah, it's also about football and sentient space probes. You can read it here, and there's also a sequel here.


Because the story is web-based, I decided to look at the HTML/CSS that was being used to see how other stories incorporate the languages in a creative way.

Whenever a character has dialogue, they are indicated with a unique text color. Each character is given a class, and their dialogue is covered in a "p" tag with the class name.

The relevant CSS file simply changes the color of the text and the padding, but it really helps tell a unique story.
